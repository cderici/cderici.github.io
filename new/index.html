Hi!

I am a professional nerd, an avid keyboard user, amateur husband, and soon to be unemployed professional brain user.

Currently I'am a PhD candidate of Computer Science at IU, specialized in Programming Languages (PL), minoring in Logic at the Deartment of Philosophy. My advisor is Sam Tobin-Hochstadt. I'm planning to graduate around mid 2020.

I like all abstractions, functions, objects, sometimes types as well. Depending on the language I often prefer tail recursion to a for-loop, continuations to exceptions, and stack to heap (who likes GC pressure, right?). Every now and then I try to use my superpowers for good of others.

I often get asked what does it mean to specialize on PL. Well the short answer is, you implement programs to solve problems, I implement languages to do that.

I prefer collaboration and team work to competition, (good) tea to (good) coffee, kindness to power. I equally and unconditionally like all the bipedals and polypods alike.

My undergraduate work was about adding tail-calls to a Racket to JS compiler. In my masters I tried the Machine Learning and the Natural Language Processing; my work was about developing a question answering system for high-school students. Currently (2019) my PhD work is about fast runtimes of dynamic languages. In particular, I'm trying to show that a very fast runtime for a self-hosting language (e.g. Racket) is achievable using a tracing JIT compiler. You can see the details for all of them down below if you're interested.

Thanks for checking out my homepage!
