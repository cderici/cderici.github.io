Many many times I find myself writing something on somewhere. I don't really love the written form of expression (e.g. no more than speaking anyway). It's like a grandpa waiting in line for his pension, awfully slow and ardous compared to speaking, let alone thinking. However, we are terrible thinkers, and I think we need this kind of practice to linger among our thoughts without losing them. Otherwise, we lose ourselves at the first corner. Just like that I prepare this website to organize and take a look at my own life, writing lets us organize and take a look at what's really going on inside.

My writing concentrates around three activities. First of all, there's the academic writing. It's unbelievably important sharing ideas for a scientific society that transcends centuries. I personally don't see it as a mere job or a responsibility in that sense, however I usually prefer different kinds of writing considering the writing experience per se.

I do not keep a diary. However, I like to keep detailed records of important events, thoughts or feelings I experienced (for some disturbingly nonsensical meaning of important). I really love to keep a journal of this sort because there's no audience, thus it really helps to understand what's going on. Otherwise you think about the reader and this empathy bomb completely ruins the experience. There're however times you need some kind of an audience to get away from yourself. I prefer to think of future myself or my future children when such a crave pops up.

Finally, my third kind of writing deals with long documents/big projects (e.g. books). I don't want to call these as "books", because first of all I'm not a "writer", therefore I don't really know how to write one, but more importantly, a book is not a one man's job. There are editors, producers, designers and all kinds of people working just to produce a text. My writings are long and somewhat organized, but they are definitely not books. Our bit somehow realizes what's going on and tries to fix it, leading only of course to an even bigger mess of things. It's really fantastic and funny to imagine this absurd story, and I believe I'm reflecting this to the writing. If you know the Hitchiker's Guide to The Galaxy, you know exactly the style that I'm talking about. It's being written in English, and I hope to finish it the next summer, we'll see.
